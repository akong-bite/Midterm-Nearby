The Disappearance of Spiritual "Nearby":
From Familiarity to Fragmentation

Spiritual "Nearby" is rooted in emotional and psychological closeness, established through interactions fostering familiarity, resonance, and shared experiences. It transcends physical proximity, relying on mutual understanding and meaningful engagement to create deep connections.

However, with the advent of technologies like the telegraph and internet, "spiritual nearby" transformed into abstract networks. Face-to-face interactions declined, disrupting traditional social circles and weakening emotional bonds. Virtual spaces replaced tangible daily engagements, fostering superficial relationships and amplifying loneliness, marking the fragmentation of spiritual intimacy into functional digital proximity.
